How this works
This calculator runs a month-by-month simulation of your actual debt payoff. Each month it applies interest to your balances, pays the minimums, then throws any extra payment at your target debt — either the highest interest rate first (Avalanche) to save the most money, or the smallest balance first (Snowball) for quick wins and momentum. The results show your real payoff timeline and total interest cost.

Important: This is an estimator, not financial advice. Numbers assume fixed rates and consistent payments. For a plan tailored to your full situation, connect with a professional.

How this works
This uses the same DTI guidelines lenders actually use. The 28% front-end rule says your housing payment shouldn't exceed 28% of gross monthly income. The 36% back-end rule says all debt payments combined shouldn't exceed 36%. We take whichever limit is more conservative and reverse-engineer a maximum home price from your numbers using standard amortization math.

Important: This is an estimator, not a pre-approval or financial advice. Your actual buying power depends on your credit score, employment history, and the full picture a lender reviews. Want a real number? Brian can introduce you to a trusted loan officer.
